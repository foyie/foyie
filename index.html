The infrastructure gap

GitHub

Most recommendation systems are static offline models. Wavelength is a continual learning engine , Thompson Sampling with Beta posteriors updates per-interaction through Kafka; two ADWIN drift detectors flag reward-distribution shifts before they compound; SPRT sequential A/B testing halts experiments the moment statistical evidence is sufficient, saving compute and avoiding false positives. Catastrophic forgetting is countered with EWC regularisation so older preferences aren't overwritten as taste evolves. The entire stack , Kafka, Redis, FastAPI, PostgreSQL, Prometheus , runs across 10 Docker services and sustains 100+ req/s with graceful degradation when Kafka is unavailable.

VeriRAG Hybrid RAG · LLM evaluation pipeline
→
View project

RAG doesn't fail loudly

GitHub

VeriRAG builds the quality infrastructure most RAG systems skip. A hybrid retrieval pipeline fuses BM25 and Qdrant dense search via Reciprocal Rank Fusion , every stage closing one of three failure modes: retrieval gap, semantic mismatch, or hallucinated generation. Cohere reranking re-orders candidates; then every answer is scored by an LLM-as-judge that classifies failures as retrieval or generation, producing a diagnostic signal rather than a pass/fail. A CI gate in the evaluation harness blocks deployment if RAGAS faithfulness or answer-relevancy drops more than 5% from baseline. 13 ablation runs across 79 golden questions yielded a key insight: a 3-sentence system prompt outperforms a 100-word one, with faithfulness = 1.0, validating per-module evaluation and real-time WandB dashboards.

2026 Probabilistic Forecasting Engine · Adaptive Conformal Prediction
ConformalCast Conformal prediction · Ensemble forecasting
→
View project

A forecast isn't a plan

GitHub

Grid operators can't act on point forecasts , they need calibrated intervals that hold under real-world distribution shift. ConformalCast uses EnbPI conformal prediction to deliver guaranteed 80% coverage on 87K PJM hourly energy records without assuming Gaussian errors. A NeuralProphet + LSTM + LightGBM ensemble provides base predictions; ensemble weighting is optimised per fold. A KS-test drift detector monitors residual distributions and auto-shrinks the calibration window when demand patterns shift , maintaining coverage when a fixed window would break. Conformal interval width serves as a live uncertainty budget. Result: 83.1% empirical coverage against the 80% target, with prediction intervals that tighten on predictable days and widen appropriately on volatile ones. A 7-day rolling-window display stops coverage from falling below 75%.

Fine-tuning is an engineering problem

GitHub

A generic support bot is a liability , it loses nuance, misroutes edge cases, and costs more than necessary. DomainRoute routes tickets to four specialised QLoRA adapters on Mistral-7B: technical, billing, returns, and escalation. An SBERT-based intent router dispatches tickets in 12ms; any ticket scoring below 0.7 confidence auto-escalates to a human agent, and the escalation adapter achieves 0% false negatives. A Pareto analysis across LoRA ranks found r=32 delivers 96.5% accuracy at $0.12 per ticket , 85% cheaper than running the full model. Semantic drift tracking confirmed cosine similarity >0.94 across all four domains, confirming adapter specialisation holds under production load. Served via FastAPI with per-domain W&B dashboards tracking real-time quality.

Credit ratings lag distress by 6 months

GitHub

This system catches distress before credit ratings do. Four signal types , SEC EDGAR financials, GDELT news sentiment through FinBERT, LinkedIn headcount velocity, and Glassdoor ratings , are fused across 100 companies × 36 months. Prophet + LSTM forecasts for slow-moving signals added 6 AUC points over the tabular-only baseline. Cox Proportional Hazards modeling adds a time-to-failure ranking alongside the binary classifier, enabling procurement teams to prioritise which distressed suppliers will fail soonest. SHAP waterfall charts explain exactly which signal fired each alert. Walk-forward backtesting across multiple folds yielded a mean AUC of 0.80, validated at p <0.01. A FastAPI + React dashboard surfaces the full risk profile for any supplier, with temporal causal charts excluding post-distress labels to prevent data leakage.

Churn models answer the wrong question

GitHub

Churn models predict who leaves , not who responds to intervention. CausalLift estimates individual treatment effects across 100K synthetic retail customers using T-Learner, X-Learner, Causal Forest, and an ensemble. Causal Forest with honest splitting delivers the most stable CATE estimates. Validation is rigorous: a placebo permutation test places the T-Learner 18.7σ above the null (p=0.000), and oracle benchmarking shows it captures 91.5% of theoretically perfect performance. Weighting gives higher-value customers more influence over treatment decisions. A Streamlit policy simulator surfaces live ROI as targeting depth, cost, and revenue sliders change , including "sleeping dog" customers a standard churn model would have targeted but whose intervention would backfire.

2025 Predictive Portfolio Intelligence System
Predictive Portfolio Regime detection · Quant backtesting
→
View project

Most backtests are lying

GitHub

Data leakage, survivorship bias, ignoring transaction costs , three ways quant backtests flatter themselves. This one avoids all three. A Hidden Markov Model regime detector labels each trading day as bull, bear, or sideways; an XGBoost model trained on macro signals (VIX, yield spread, credit spreads) and technicals (RSI, MACD) predicts 10-day forward returns and drives dynamic allocation. Walk-forward backtesting with explicit per-asset transaction costs cuts gross returns of 5% to a realistic net 3.2%. Outperformance vs 60/40 is validated with a paired t-test (p=0.031). Regime-specific performance across 7 distinct market conditions is reported, and SHAP values translate predictions into plain-language allocation rationale per regime. Transaction costs modelled explicitly via bid-ask spreading and slippage , gross vs net Sharpe: 0.56 vs 0.35 per year.

2023 Salary Prediction & Equity Analysis
PayLens ETL pipeline · Equity analytics
→
View project

Clean data is the foundation

GitHub

Raw salary data is almost unusable , mixed pay periods, duplicate records, inconsistent job titles, missing values. PayLens runs 6,700+ records through a five-stage ETL: fuzzy deduplication (Levenshtein distance), multi-strategy imputation matched to variable type, salary period normalisation, IQR outlier removal at 3× multiplier, and a priority-ordered keyword taxonomy that classifies job fields consistently. Validation at each stage uses holdout sets. The cleaned dataset feeds a four-panel interactive Tableau dashboard with filters across education, job field, and experience level , revealing that PhD holders carry the widest salary spread, that high-salary segments skew technical and managerial, and that 3–9 years of experience unlocks the steepest premium and unstructured job titles are the dominant source of misclassification.

    C. Das, S. S. Gokulnath, A. Gaikwad, K. Senthilnathan, S. P. Sawant · UCSD

    Abstract

    Detection of 206 bird and wildlife species from noisy long-form field audio with severe class imbalance (some species <5 training examples). Rather than a single multi-label model, we trained one dedicated binary XGBoost classifier per species, encoding audio as compact binary frequency activation vectors from STFT plus log-mel statistics and geographic metadata. Targeted augmentation (pitch shift, time stretch, gain) lifted 31 zero-F1 species , some reaching F1 = 0.72. Public leaderboard score: 0.568, competitive with transformer baselines (YAMNet/AST ~0.55–0.56) with no GPU required.

    RavNet: Cascaded U-Net for Retinal Artery and Vein Segmentation

    C. Das, S. Ghosh · IEM Kolkata / UEM

    ,
    ▾
    ↗

    Abstract

    Cascaded two-model U-Net architecture for automated retinal artery/vein segmentation. The first model performs binary vessel detection; its mask is overlaid onto the original image and fed to a second model with three specialized decoders (arteries, veins, auxiliary reconstruction). Combined BCE + Dice + MSE loss. Evaluated on the RAVIR dataset (46 high-res SLO images, UCLA Stein Eye Institute): Dice 0.779 (arteries), 0.789 (veins), vein specificity 0.993 , outperforming U-Net, Dense U-Net, Residual U-Net, and R2U-Net.
